How I Got here

My interest in web development began at an early age with my first computer class as a Freshman in High School, 2007. We went over basic HTML and CSS. I quickly fell in love with the idea of being able to create something extraordinary out of text. The customization of every little detail was fascinating.

In 2014, I createdStarr Management, a talent agency. This is where I found WIX. I was still very new to coding, and the WIX platform offered a versatile interface for customizing a website, creating forms, and interacting with visitors. I loved it. I learned a lot about layouts, themes, and the limitations of html components. But, as my business grew, I quickly ran into customization issues with WIX. I wanted my clients to be able to log in and view upcoming gigs, accept gigs, maintain their schedule, access documents, and communicate with me directly. I knew these things had to be possible, but the functionality was not available at the time.

In 2017 I shut down Starr Management and created TW Auto LLC: a mobile mechanic shop. This being my second company, I understood the fundamentals of setting up shop and what I was going to need to advertise to the community. The business exploded after 6 months of opening. This was my first experience with hiring employees, managing payroll, higher invoice amounts, and a multitude of expenses. I quickly realized that I was not able to run everything solo as I did with Starr Management. I utilized the WIX platform again, since it was familiar to me, but was wanting to stretch the limits of its capabilities to offer more convenience and services to my customers.

In 2018, I hired a marketing team to design my website. We worked closely together and I became very involved in the expansion of my website. Unfortunately, WIX was still not able to provide the functionality that I was looking for. This led me to look for third-party applications to compensate. I found Mitchel 1. This platform was customized to stationary mechanic shops, not mobile mechanic shops. This led to significant work-arounds and the use of several third-party applications to provide the services that I desired for my employees and customers.

In 2020, I went through a very difficult divorce, had to close down TW Auto LLC, and start completely over. This gave me the time and opportunity to focus on what I wanted to do next with my career. I wanted to bring back TW Auto LLC, but run it more efficiently and with a more solid foundation. With less money and resources than when I started TW Auto LLC, it led me into several months of planning and going over how I could improve things on my next go around. I realized that I would need to automate many of duties and put more control into the hands of my customers and employees, as well as being highly accessible. I needed to create a responsive web application.

In 2022 I began researching and learning as much as I could about web development. I found Coursera.com and fell in love with the variety of courses that were available to me. I enrolled in the IBM Full Stack Software Developer Program. This program refreshed my memory on the core concepts of HTML and CSS, as well as extended my knowledge of front-end development with LESS, SASS, and JavaScript. The program not only delved into backend development with Django, NodeJS, and React, but taught DevOps, twelve-factor app development, and many open-source libraries to utilize. My passion for app development was aflame and I was no longer focused on re-creating TW Auto LLC but learning and growing as a software developer.

Now, in 2023, I am creating responsive web applications focused on small businesses, utilizing my experience as a small business owner to customize the needs of each individual client. I am currently expanding my portfolio to showcase these skills and building base templates for common needs of a business owner.
